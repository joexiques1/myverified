About Us

The AI World can be dangerous and uncertain.

MyVerified is dedicated to creating a digital future based on authenticity and confidence. We create safety, creator control, and real human connection.

MyVerified is a conversation platform

by which content creators of all types can build and train AI-powered digital personas of themselves designed to interact with followers on a 24/7 basis, simultaneously expanding their reach to new users and deepening relationships with their existing fan bases.

Creators range from celebrities and influencers, for whom MyVerified is additive to their current media footprint, to people who don't have any media presence but have interesting skills or content they'd like to share. MyVerified takes content, which historically has been delivered to audiences in a one-way direction, and makes that content come alive in two-way personalized conversations between the artist/teacher/author and the fan.

The platform allows creators to monetize their content in a variety of ways — from viewership on the platform itself to promoting and curating existing revenue streams, which they can market and target to individual followers with whom they're creating more personalized relationships through their interactions on the platform.

For consumers, MyVerified only allows approved and verifiable creators to participate, so followers know they're having unique and private conversations with the authentic owner of the content in which they're interested. For creators, MyVerified enables them to build, monitor, manage, and own their digital personas, so they control, leverage, and benefit from the AI revolution.

Our Team

Founder and Co-President

Ruben Rodriguez

For more than four decades, Ruben Rodriguez has helped redefine how Hollywood works behind the scenes. A CPA by training but a technologist at heart, Ruben has been at the center of the entertainment industry's most important operational and financial transformations.

Starting at CBS in internal audit, he quickly moved into roles that allowed him to understand both the creative and financial sides of the business. At Entertainment Partners, he worked on major features like Dick Tracy, gaining firsthand experience with the complexities of production accounting. His leadership later took him to Western Costume as CFO, where he modernized financial operations at one of the industry's most storied companies.

Ruben then co-founded Ease Entertainment Services, determined to replace the industry's outdated paper workflows with modern, digital tools. Under his vision, Ease launched technologies—including what became SmartAccounting—that changed how studios, production companies, and accountants operate. These innovations saved time and money while setting new standards for compliance, transparency, and collaboration. Ease ultimately completed a nine-figure sale.

Following the acquisition, Ruben served as Chief of Staff at Entertainment Partners and later as CTO at For The Record, where he led the development of virtual courtroom technology ahead of its time.

Throughout his career, Ruben's mission has remained constant: solve complex problems, build transformative systems, and move the entertainment industry forward.

Founder and Co-President

Valerie Stadler

Valerie Stadler is a producer, filmmaker and executive whose films have screened at Sundance, SXSW, Tribeca, MOCA, TIFF and the Whitney Museum. Driven by a strong sense of storytelling and understanding of the industry, Valerie navigates the line between business and creative.

Valerie is a Co-Founder of Big Swing Productions with Kyra Sedgwick and Meredith Bagby, where she has developed a slate of projects across film and television, with an emphasis on stories with heart and purpose. Prior to that, Valerie was the Head of Production and Development for Fluency, a multi-platform studio under NBC Universal, handling both day-to-day operations and development of the slate, as well as fostering partnerships both inside and outside of the studio.

Valerie is a 2019 Sundance Episodic lab fellow, on the Outfest and Seriesfest Board of Directors, and lives in Los Angeles with her husband and one too many cats.

Founder and Chairman of the Board

Laurie Segall

Laurie Segall is an award-winning journalist, media executive, and founder of Mostly Human Media, a New York-based entertainment company focused on society and artificial intelligence. Formerly CNN's senior technology correspondent and a reporter for 60 Minutes.

Laurie has spent over 15 years covering the intersection of tech and humanity — interviewing the world's most influential tech leaders, including Mark Zuckerberg and Tim Cook, and giving audiences a behind-the-scenes look at companies like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Uber during their rise.

She is the author of Special Characters: My Adventures with Tech's Titans and Misfits, a memoir chronicling her front-row seat to the tech boom. Her work has earned her a Gracie Award for Revenge Porn: The War on Women and a Webby Award for My Deepfake Relationship with Mark Zuckerberg.

Laurie is a graduate of the University of Michigan and is based in New York.

Chief Strategy Officer

Michael Rose

Michael Rose is a seasoned entrepreneur and executive with years of leadership across technology, operations, and strategy. He co-founded Ease Entertainment Services and grew it from a five-person startup into an industry leader with more than 170 employees across 11 offices, pioneering software and ACA compliance programs that set the standard for the entertainment staffing industry and ultimately selling the company for over nine figures.

Michael later led Dunn-Edwards Paints, where he quadrupled the company's value through disciplined strategy and organizational leadership. He currently serves as Chairman of the Board of For The Record (FTR), a global technology company that provides digital recording and cloud-based solutions to modernize how court proceedings are captured, managed, and accessed, advancing accuracy, transparency, and accessibility in the justice system.

Earlier in his career, Michael was an Engagement Manager at McKinsey & Company, advising Fortune 500 executives on strategy and profit improvement, and held leadership roles at Procter & Gamble. He holds a Bachelor of Arts in History from Northwestern University and an MBA with Distinction from Harvard Business School.

Michael is also deeply committed to public service and has held board leadership roles with the National Paint and Coatings Association, Outfest, and Stories: The AIDS Monument in West Hollywood.

Project Manager

Yaron Spiwak

Yaron Spiwak is the Project Manager at MyVerified, where he oversees project timelines, testing processes, production workflows, and cross-team coordination. He plays a key role in translating ideas into execution, supporting teams with production planning, operational structure, and day-to-day logistical problem solving to ensure the platform evolves efficiently and reliably.

Yaron brings extensive experience at the intersection of creativity, technology, and large-scale production. Alongside his production leadership work, Yaron has maintained an active career in music, collaborating with artists including Brandy, Luis Fonsi, Lindsey Stirling, and Todrick Hall. These projects further strengthened his ability to manage creative teams, technical systems, and operational stakeholders in fast-moving, performance-driven environments.

In addition to his role at MyVerified, Yaron works as an AI consultant for Wubble.ai, a music technology company focused on next-generation AI music intelligence. His experience evaluating and shaping emerging AI tools informs MyVerified's approach to building creator-first systems that balance innovation, trust, and authenticity.

Yaron's background in high-stakes production, cross-company collaboration, and emerging AI platforms allows him to bridge vision and execution—supporting MyVerified's mission to build meaningful, verified digital identities.
